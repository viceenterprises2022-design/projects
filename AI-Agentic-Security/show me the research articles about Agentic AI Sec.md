## Core “State of AI Agent Security” reports

### 1. Gravitee – State of AI Agent Security 2026

- Survey of 900+ execs and practitioners on AI agent deployment, security approval, and incidents.[^1][^2]
- Key data points:
    - 80.9–81% of teams are past planning into testing/production.[^1]
    - Only 14.4% have full security/IT approval for their agent fleet.[^1]
    - On average only ~47% of agents are actively monitored or secured.[^1]
    - 88% of organizations report confirmed or suspected agent‑related incidents; >90% in healthcare.[^1]
- Key narrative: adoption has outpaced governance; the primary issue is loss of control and identity, not “hallucinations”.[^2][^1]

**Links:**

- Gravitee blog article (summary + key charts).[^1]
- Gravitee “State of AI Agent Security” landing page (full report download).[^2]

***

### 2. NeuralTrust – The State of AI Agent Security 2026

- Vendor–neutral style report based on 160+ CISOs and security leaders.[^3]
- Findings:
    - 72% have implemented or are scaling AI agents, but only 29% report comprehensive security controls.[^3]
    - 73% of CISOs are very/critically concerned about AI risk; only ~30% have mature safeguards.[^3]
    - At least 1 in 5 organizations report an AI‑agent–related breach (prompt injection, data exposure, etc.).[^3]
- Emphasis on treating agents as first‑class security principals and moving from static controls to continuous, behavior‑aware defenses.[^3]

***

### 3. Arkose Labs – 2026 Agentic AI Security Report

- Survey of 300 enterprise leaders (incl. Fortune 100) on AI‑agent risk.[^4]
- Calls out gaps across readiness, detection, attribution, insider risk, and budget allocation to AI‑agent risk.[^4]
- Headline stats:
    - 97% of enterprise leaders expect a material AI‑agent incident within 12 months.[^4]
    - Growing share of security budgets earmarked for agentic AI risk specifically.[^4]

***

### 4. “85% Adopted, 88% Breached” industry article

- Synthesizes multiple surveys:
    - 80.9–85% of enterprises have adopted AI agents in some form.[^5]
    - 88% have experienced security incidents related to agents, while only 14.4% have full production deployment with complete approval.[^5]
    - Confirms the “adoption‑control gap”: executives are confident, but only ~47% of agents are actually monitored.[^5]

***

### 5. AI Agent Security overview (AICerts / others)

- Explains AI Agent Security as a distinct discipline and aggregates survey data:
    - Gartner projection: 40% of enterprise applications will embed task‑specific agents by late 2026 (up from 5%).[^6]
    - 82% of respondents already run agents; only 44% enforce dedicated policies; 23% have seen credential leakage after prompt injection.[^6]

## Gartner‑aligned projections and AI security platform forecasts

Gartner content is mostly behind paywalls, but their key forecasts are widely quoted and summarized.

### 6. Gartner – AI security platforms \& incident response (2028+)

Several summaries capture Gartner’s 2026 predictions about AI security platforms and AI‑driven incident response:

- **By 2028, more than 50% of enterprises will use AI security platforms** to secure third‑party AI usage and custom AI applications, addressing prompt injection, data misuse, and policy enforcement.[^7][^8][^9]
- **By 2028, 50% of cybersecurity incident response efforts will focus on incidents involving custom‑built AI applications**, as organizations deploy AI before fully testing or securing it.[^8][^9]
- **Through 2030, 33% of IT work will be spent remediating AI data debt** to secure AI, as poorly structured/secured data becomes a major bottleneck.[^7][^8]
- **By 2028, 70% of CISOs will use identity visibility and intelligence capabilities** to shrink the IAM attack surface, driven by growth in human and machine (agent) identities.[^8][^7]
- Manual AI compliance processes are expected to leave 75% of regulated orgs exposed to fines >5% of global revenue by 2027, pushing demand for automated AI risk/compliance platforms.[^9][^8]

These are summarized in pieces like:

- DigitalTerminal / CISO ET coverage of Gartner’s 2026 AI \& security predictions.[^7][^8]
- Kiteworks “Custom AI cybersecurity incidents: Gartner’s 2028 forecast” analysis.[^9]

### 7. Gartner – Agentic AI spending \& “securing AI” vs “AI‑amplified security”

- Forecasts cited in a 2026 agentic AI forecast roundup:
    - Agentic AI spending projected at **\$201.9B in 2026**, overtaking chatbot spending by 2027.[^10]
    - AI cybersecurity market projected at **\$51.3B in 2026**, roughly doubling from 2025.[^10]
    - By 2029, “AI‑amplified security” reaches \$160.4B, while “securing AI” as a category is only \$11.6B—showing current spending skews toward using AI defensively rather than securing AI systems themselves.[^10]

This is useful context for why “securing AI” (Pinaka’s space) is under‑served despite huge macro spend.

### 8. Agentic AI market size \& CAGR

Beyond Gartner, there are adjacent market forecasts:

- Fortune Business Insights estimates the global **agentic AI market** at **\$7.29B in 2025**, growing to **\$139.19B by 2034** at a **40.5% CAGR**, with security and surveillance as one of the core application segments.[^11]
