No. 02 · Philosophy

Tea is not a beverage. It is a pause — the smallest room in which a person can remember who they are. We do not sell leaves. We send twenty quiet minutes, four times each year, wrapped carefully in paper.

From the founder's letter · Spring 2024
No. 03 · The Selection

Four teas.
Four seasons.
One attention.

Spring · 春 玉露

Gyokuro,
shaded three weeks

Grown in the slow mist of Uji valley, picked by hand in the final week of April. Steeps into a pale jade liquor — sweet marine, soft grass, a long finish of chestnut skin.

Summer · 夏 焙じ茶

Hōjicha,
slow-roasted in cedar

Autumn-pruned stems, roasted over low cedar embers until the leaf turns mahogany. Warm, low-caffeine, with notes of toasted rice, dried fig, and woodsmoke at dusk.

Winter · 冬 抹茶

Matcha,
stone-milled at dawn

Tencha leaves milled between granite wheels at the cold hour before sunrise — yielding thirty grams in ninety minutes. Whisked bright, vegetal, with an umami depth that lingers quietly.

No. 04 · The Vessel

Each quarter,
a new cup arrives —
thrown by one pair of hands.

We partner with three lineage kilns across Japan — Hagi, Bizen, and Mashiko — to commission a small run of tea bowls each season. Every vessel is signed, numbered, and sent with a letter describing its making: the clay, the firing, the potter's hand.

  • Material Unwashed local clay, wood-ash glaze
  • Firing Noborigama, four days & four nights
  • Packaging Paulownia box, washi, braided cord
  • Edition Fifty vessels per season, never repeated
